# Indicators

Indicators are mathematical calculations plotted on charts. They help identify trends, momentum, volatility, and potential reversals.

**But indicators lag price.** They describe what's happened, not what's coming.

---

## Indicator Categories

### Trend Indicators

Identify direction of market movement.

| Indicator | Best For |
|-----------|---------|
| SMA/EMA | Trend direction |
| Ichimoku | Multi-factor view |
| Parabolic SAR | Trend reversal |
| ADX | Trend strength |

---

### Momentum Indicators

Measure speed of price movement.

| Indicator | Best For |
|-----------|---------|
| RSI | Overbought/oversold |
| MACD | Trend momentum |
| Stochastic | Crossovers |
| ROC | Rate of change |

---

### Volatility Indicators

Measure rate of price movement.

| Indicator | Best For |
|-----------|---------|
| ATR | How much price moves |
| Bollinger Bands | Range/contraction |
| Keltner Channel | Volatility bands |
| Standard Deviation | Variance |

---

### Volume Indicators

Measure trade activity.

| Indicator | Best For |
|-----------|---------|
| Volume | Raw activity |
| OBV | Cumulative flow |
| VWAP | Fair value |
| MFI | Money flow |

---

## When to Use Each

### Trend Indicators

**Use when:**
- Market is trending (not ranging)
- You need directional bias
- You're trend following

**Don't use when:**
- Market is ranging
- No clear direction
- Choppy conditions

---

### Momentum Indicators

**Use when:**
- You want overbought/oversold levels
- Looking for divergences
- Trading reversals

**Don't use when:**
- Strong trending move (RSI stays overbought)
- Choppy markets (false signals)

---

### Volatility Indicators

**Use when:**
- Sizing stops
- Planning target distance
- Identifying breakout potential

**Don't use when:**
- Need directional signals
- Using in isolation

---

### Volume Indicators

**Use when:**
- Confirming breakouts
- Identifying accumulation/distribution
- Checking sustainability

**Don't use when:**
- Low-volume markets
- Alone for entries

---

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Using Without Context

**Problem:** RSI shows overbought but price keeps rising.

**Why:** Strong trends stay overbought.

**Fix:** Use in context of market structure.

---

### 2. Too Many Indicators

**Problem:** 10 indicators, no clarity.

**Fix:** 2-3 maximum. Each serves one purpose.

---

### 3. Chasing Signals

**Problem:** Entry on signal, no context.

**Fix:** Confirm with structure first, then signal.

---

## Indicator Setup Template

```
Trend: 50 SMA, 200 SMA
Momentum: RSI (14)
Context: Daily chart for structure
```

---

# Key Takeaways

1. **Indicators lag** — They describe past, not future
2. **Use 2-3 max** — More = confusion
3. **Match to strategy** — Trend strategy = trend indicators
4. **Confirm with structure** — Don't enter on indicator alone
5. **Adjust to conditions** — Different tools for different markets
