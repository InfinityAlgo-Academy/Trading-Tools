# What Is Trading

Trading is the art and science of buying and selling financial instruments to profit from price movements. It's not gambling — it's a skill that can be learned, practiced, and improved.

---

## The Core Concept

At its simplest, trading means:

1. **Buy low** — Purchase an asset expecting the price to rise
2. **Sell high** — Sell that asset at a higher price
3. **Profit** — Keep the difference

The challenge? Knowing when to buy and when to sell.

---

## Why Trading Works

Markets move because of:

- **Supply and demand** — More buyers than sellers = price goes up
- **Information** — News and events change sentiment
- **Emotion** — Fear and greed drive price swings
- **Trends** — Prices tend to continue in their direction

Traders exploit these patterns.

---

## Types of Trading

| Type | Timeframe | Style |
|-----|-----------|-------|
| Scalping | Seconds to minutes | Quick trades, small profits |
| Day Trading | Minutes to hours | Close all positions daily |
| Swing Trading | Days to weeks | Hold through price swings |
| Position Trading | Weeks to months | Long-term trends |

---

## What Traders Actually Do

1. **Analyze** — Study charts, news, and data
2. **Plan** — Define entry, exit, and risk rules
3. **Execute** — Place trades with defined risk
4. **Review** — Learn from each trade

---

# Is Trading Right for You?

Trading requires:

- **Time** — To learn and practice
- **Capital** — To risk (never more than you can afford to lose)
- **Discipline** — To follow your plan
- **Patience** — To wait for the right setup

If you're willing to put in the work, trading can be a valuable skill.
